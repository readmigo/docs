# Comprehensive Guide to Open Source, Creative Commons, and Public Domain Books in Business, Management, and Economics

This research report provides a curated collection of legally free, high-quality educational resources suitable for a reading application.

---

## 1. Economics

### 1.1 Classical Economics (Public Domain)

| Title | Author | License/Status | Source | Description | Quality |
|-------|--------|----------------|--------|-------------|---------|
| **An Inquiry into the Nature and Causes of the Wealth of Nations** | Adam Smith | Public Domain | [Project Gutenberg #3300](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/3300) | The foundational text of modern economics (1776), examining labor productivity, division of labor, capital accumulation, and the role of commerce | Excellent - Foundational text |
| **Das Kapital (Capital)** | Karl Marx | Public Domain | [Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/capitalvol1) / [Wikisource](https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Das_Kapital) | Critique of political economy (1867), analyzing capitalism, commodities, money, surplus-value, and accumulation | Excellent - Seminal work |
| **Principles of Political Economy** | John Stuart Mill | Public Domain | [Project Gutenberg #30107](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/30107) | Comprehensive 1848 textbook on production, distribution, and government's economic role | Excellent - Classical synthesis |
| **On the Principles of Political Economy and Taxation** | David Ricardo | Public Domain | [Online Library of Liberty](https://oll.libertyfund.org/titles/ricardo-the-works-and-correspondence-of-david-ricardo-vol-1-principles-of-political-economy-and-taxation) | Analysis of allocation between capitalists, landowners, and workers; advocacy for free trade | Excellent - Trade theory foundation |
| **Principles of Economics** | Alfred Marshall | Public Domain | [Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/ost-business-marshall_0197_ebk_v60) | Influential neoclassical economics textbook | Excellent - Neoclassical foundation |

## 6. Quality Indicators Explained

| Rating | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Excellent** | Peer-reviewed, widely adopted at universities, regularly updated |
| **Very Good** | Faculty-reviewed, comprehensive coverage, reliable content |
| **Good** | Useful content, may be specialized or less frequently updated |
